The Problem

Marine electronics are stuck in the past.

NMEA2000 is a powerful protocol — it connects depth sounders, wind instruments, GPS units, engine sensors, and more into a single shared network. It's the backbone of nearly every modern yacht's electronics system.

But accessing that data? That's where things fall apart. The hardware is overpriced. The software is locked down. Every manufacturer guards their ecosystem, leaving boat owners dependent on expensive, proprietary tools just to see what their own sensors are reporting.

What Yachtmesh Does

Everything your NMEA2000 network deserves.

🔌

Plug-and-Play Hardware

Connect to your NMEA2000 network in minutes. No proprietary gateways, no complex setup — just plug in and start reading data.

🌐

Works Across All Brands

Garmin, Raymarine, Simrad, B&G — it doesn't matter. Yachtmesh reads standard NMEA2000 data from any manufacturer's sensors.

🔓

Fully Open Source

Hardware schematics, firmware, and software — all open. Inspect it, modify it, contribute to it. Your boat, your data, your code.

👨‍💻

Developer Friendly

REST APIs, WebSocket streams, MQTT support. Integrate your boat data with Home Assistant, Signal K, custom dashboards, or anything you build.

📱

Modern Software Interface

A clean, responsive web dashboard that runs on any device. Monitor your boat from a tablet at the helm or a phone on shore.

⛵

Built for Real Sailors

Designed by someone who actually lives aboard. Every feature is informed by real-world use — not a spec sheet written in an office.
